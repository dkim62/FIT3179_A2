Australia Road Safety

From 2017 to 2021, although there has been a minor decrease in average number of total car accidents, there are still 60 to 120 accidents happening every month in Australia.

The line chart below shows the number of reported car accidents by states and a total sum of them all in a thick blue line. These accidents happen all across the land of Australia and a detail incident rate by each Local Government Area (LGA) can be found on the map shown right. The incident rate is a percentage of reported case of a certain LGA to the total number of accidents happened in the same year.
In summary, there has been approximately 4,700 fatal car accidents over last 5 years. The summarized bar chart above shows classified records of these accidents by categories.
For crash type, single type accidents happen 14.8% more than the multiple type. Drivers are reported to be dominantly injured or died from the accidents by almost 49% among all the road users. For different age group, a group of people with age of 40 to 64 are reported to be reponsible by 32% and the accidents happen more often on Friday to Sunday relative to other weekdays with a peak at Saturday.
Lastly, a major percentage of car accidents involved male road users. (76.6%) However, this is an absolute number of the total accidents and does not indicate the rate.
